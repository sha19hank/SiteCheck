# Core Product Vision

## What SiteCheck IS
SiteCheck is an AI-powered Website Growth Consultant.

Its purpose is to evaluate how effectively a website fulfills its intended purpose and help owners improve the website's usability, clarity, trust, interaction, accessibility, conversion, and overall visitor experience.

SiteCheck improves websites.
It does not attempt to become a business strategy consultant.

## What SiteCheck is NOT
The engine only reasons over observable website evidence. SiteCheck explicitly does NOT provide advice on:
* Company strategy
* Hiring advice
* Funding advice
* Product roadmap
* Market expansion
* Sales organization
* Pricing strategy beyond what is visible on the website
* Internal business operations

## Core Philosophy
The engine must always follow this reasoning pipeline:

```
Understand Website
↓
Understand Purpose
↓
Understand Visitor
↓
Understand Desired Action
↓
Understand Current Experience
↓
Identify Friction
↓
Determine Highest Impact Improvements
↓
Generate Evidence-backed Recommendations
```

Recommendations are the consequence of understanding. They are NOT the starting point.

## Primary User Personas
SiteCheck is built for:
* **Solo Founders / Indie Hackers**: Need affordable, actionable advice to optimize their landing pages without hiring an agency.
* **SaaS Builders**: Need to reduce friction in their onboarding and trial sign-up flows.
* **Portfolio Owners / Creators**: Want to look professional and build trust quickly.
* **Agencies**: Need white-labeled initial audits to pitch redesigns to clients.
* **Ecommerce Owners**: Need to fix checkout friction and optimize product page clarity.
* **Nonprofits & Local Businesses**: Want their contact info and mission to be easily accessible.
* **Students / Vibe Coders**: Need quick feedback on side projects to ensure they meet modern standards.

## Success Metric
The project succeeds if, after reading the report, the website owner clearly understands:
* What is working
* What is hurting the website
* Why it matters
* What should be improved first
* How to verify the improvement

The project does NOT succeed simply by generating a high quantity of recommendations.

## Recommendation Principles
If a recommendation cannot answer these six questions, it should not exist:
1. What evidence produced this?
2. Why is it a problem (or strength)?
3. Why does it matter for THIS website?
4. How does it affect the website's purpose?
5. What is the highest impact fix?
6. How can the owner measure success afterwards?

## Project Rules
* Never hallucinate evidence.
* Never recommend something already present.
* Never optimize for quantity over quality.
* Every recommendation must be contextual.
* Website purpose determines recommendation relevance.
* Better to say "Insufficient evidence" than invent advice.
* Trust is more valuable than quantity.
